# Understanding Income and Taxes

Income can be derived from various sources, including:

- **Employment:** Wages, salaries, bonuses, and commissions
- **Self-employment:** Income from running a business or freelancing
- **Investments:** Dividends, interest, capital gains, and rental income
- **Government benefits:** Social Security, disability, unemployment, etc.

## Gross Income vs. Net Income

- **Gross income:** The total amount of money you earn before taxes and deductions.
- **Net income:** Also known as take-home pay, is the amount of money you receive after taxes and other deductions (e.g., insurance premiums, retirement contributions) have been subtracted from your gross income.

## Paycheck Deductions

Paycheck deductions are amounts withheld from your gross pay by your employer. Common deductions include:

- Federal and state income taxes
- Social Security and Medicare contributions
- Health insurance premiums
- Retirement plan contributions

## Income Tax

Income tax is a tax levied by federal, state, and local governments on various types of income. Tax filing involves completing and submitting the appropriate tax forms (e.g., Form 1040 for federal income tax) and claiming eligible deductions and credits to reduce your taxable income.

## Tax Planning Strategies

Tax planning strategies involve legal methods to minimize your tax liability, such as:

- Maximizing tax-deductible contributions
- Taking advantage of tax credits
- Timing income and expenses strategically
